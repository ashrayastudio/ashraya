Recall · Privacy Policy

Recall Privacy Policy (2026-07-19)

What data Recall collects, where it is stored, and who can access it.

What Recall is

Recall is a private, voice-first memory capture app made by Ashraya Studio. This policy covers what data Recall collects, where it's stored, and who can access it.

Data Recall collects

Memory content you create. Titles, details, categories, and any people/place tags you add when you capture a memory — by voice or by typing.

Voice input. Recall uses Apple's Speech framework to convert your spoken words into text when you capture a memory by voice. Recall does not save or cache the raw audio recording — audio is streamed directly to Apple's transcription system as you speak, and only the resulting text is saved as your memory. When your device supports on-device transcription, Recall requires it; otherwise, transcription may be processed by Apple's speech recognition service rather than entirely on your device.

Location: not currently collected. Recall does not access your device's location in any way.

Account/purchase information. If you subscribe to a paid tier, purchases are processed entirely by Apple through StoreKit 2 — Recall never sees or stores your payment details.

Email address, only if you invite family. If you share a memory with a family member, Recall uses your device's own Mail app to send the invite, which means providing their email address to address that message. This happens through your device's mail composer, not a server Recall controls or stores addresses on.

Where your data lives

On your device by default. Memory content is stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework, protected at the file level so it's inaccessible while your device is locked.

iCloud sync, only if you turn it on. Recall does not sync to iCloud automatically. If you enable sync, your memories are backed up to your personal iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit, in your own private database — Ashraya Studio does not have access to it.

Family Sharing, only for memories you explicitly share. If you share a memory with a family member, that specific memory (not your whole library) is shared via Apple's CloudKit sharing, directly between your iCloud account and theirs.

What Recall does not do

  • No third-party analytics or advertising SDKs. Recall has zero third-party dependencies of any kind. There is nothing in the app capable of sending your data to an outside company.
  • No photo library access. Recall does not request or use access to your Photos library.
  • No account required to start. You can use Recall without creating an account or signing in with anything beyond your own device.

Security

  • Recall supports an optional app lock using Face ID, Touch ID, or your device passcode. This authentication happens entirely through Apple's system frameworks — Recall never has access to your biometric data itself, only a yes/no confirmation that you authenticated.
  • When Recall is running in the background, a privacy screen covers your content in the App Switcher preview.

Data deletion

Deleting a memory removes it from your device and, if it was previously synced, from iCloud as well — including if you had temporarily turned sync off before deleting.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the date at the top will be updated.
